Gavin Baker — Sohn Hearts & Minds Australia 2021 stock pick: Coinbase Global (COIN)

At the 2021 Sohn Hearts & Minds conference (Australia), Gavin Baker, CIO and Managing
Partner of Atreides Management (a ~$4.5bn technology/consumer crossover firm), presented
Coinbase Global (COIN) as his single stock pick. He framed it as a "user-friendly" way to
own the growth of the crypto ecosystem as more people enter the crypto world.

Core thesis — "the AWS of crypto." Baker said he was most excited about Coinbase Cloud,
which he likened to the Amazon Web Services of crypto. As reported: "Cloud is the
infrastructure and tooling layer for web 3.0 application development on different
blockchains, and these applications are going to be very disruptive to the world of
technology over the next 20 to 30 years." He argued Coinbase Cloud positions the company as
a foundational infrastructure provider for web3, not merely a retail trading exchange.

Disruption outlook. Baker expected significantly more technological disruption driven by
the crypto world over the coming decades than markets experienced in the prior ten years —
a period he characterized as relying on a comparatively "stable" technology environment
dominated by cloud, AI, and SaaS. In his view, programmable blockchains open a much larger
and more disruptive design space.

Revenue-model argument. Baker emphasized the programmable nature of cryptocurrency, which he
said "lets a company like Coinbase generate a wide variety of revenue streams that are
simply not present with brokers that focus on fiat currency." The point: unlike a
traditional fiat-focused broker, a programmable-crypto platform can monetize across many
layers (trading, custody, staking, infrastructure/cloud, developer tooling), giving Coinbase
optionality on multiple emerging web3 revenue lines.

Positioning. The pick was long Coinbase (COIN), a high-conviction, long-term (20–30 year
horizon) bet on crypto infrastructure and web3 adoption, with Coinbase Cloud as the primary
value driver rather than near-term retail trading volumes.
